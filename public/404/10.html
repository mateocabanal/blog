Chapter 10

Questions

In the previous chapter, the boys have mistaken Simon for the beast and had brutally murdered him with their bare hands. As a result, the boys are in denial of the murdering of Simon and further show degeneration of beliefs, behavior, relationships, rules, and order. The first instance of changed behavior is when piggy claimed that they “left the feast early, because we were tired” (Golding, 120), showing that he cannot accept he was part of the murder.

The title of the chapter: “The Shell and the Glasses” is quite significant and a spoiler for the content of the chapter. The shell is referring to democracy, and the glasses are referring to intelligence. In hindsight, the chapter had been obvious about the democracy and intelligence on the island is symbolically destroyed using the evidence from the past chapters and the current mindset the boys have.
