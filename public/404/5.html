Chapter 5

Questions

During chapter 5, the children on the island experience huge changes in their beliefs, behavior, rules, and order on the island. Firstly, their belief on the island is their biggest change. The first instance of their change is on page 61: ” Unless we get frightened of people” (Golding, 61). This sudden fear is caused by the beast they have created in their minds forming in paranoia. Later, Simon states ” What I mean is…maybe its only us” (Golding, 65), and the children call blasphemy upon his words. The thought of the beast’s existence has infected the island with fear despite the little evidence of the beast they have. The change in belief causes fears to take over the kid’s reason. Secondly, the behavior on the island has also changed. Firstly, the littluns behavior had changed from rarely crying to Percival’s “tears [leaping] from his eyes, his mouth opened till they could see a black hole.” (Golding,63), because of the remembrance of their past lives. In other words, the thought of the past lives possibly being gone forever had only settled in now. Additionally, the behavior on the island had also led to some problems with the rules and order on the land regarding Jack and Ralph: ” And you shut up! Who are you anyway? Sitting there telling people what to do. You can’t hunt you can’t sing- ” (Golding, 67), “Bollocks to the rules! We’re strong - we hunt! If there’s a beast, we’ll hunt it down!” (Golding, 67). Furthermore, this little quarrel has many potential forms of foreshadowing about the future chapters and the bigger changes that will happen. In conclusion, the civilized children the boys once were, are soon to disappear into who knows what.
