Chapter 3

Questions

Once Ralph is appointed as the chief of the groups, he establishes two main priorities: Rescue and shelter. However, Jack ignores Ralph’s priorities to fuel his bloodthirst. First, when Ralph and Simon recall the meeting saying “’ Remember how everyone was to work hard until the shelter was finished?’” (Golding, 35), Jack responds “’ Except me and my hunters.’” (Golding, 35). Jack isn’t concerned about the group, instead, he is selfish by feeding his bloodthirst through hunting expressed in the first chapter: “’ Next time there will be no mercy.’” (Golding, 20). Secondly, Jack is completely ignorant of the main priority of everyone on this island: Rescue. Jack had been so consumed in feeding his bloodthirst he has already adapted to the island. “Jack had to think for a moment before he could remember what rescue was.” (Golding,37). Once Jack remembers rescue, he insanely says “’ Rescue? Yes, of course! All the same, I’d like to catch a pig first’” (Golding, 38), which shows Jack is losing touch with his normal self, transforming into a savage. Due to this metamorphosis, Jack has no interest in the group’s welfare because of his evolution to savagery.

On the island, most of the children run off into panic searching for a way to get rescued or ignore the situation they are in. However, Simon is an exception, comparing the way he and the other boys react to the island. Furthermore, Simon is the only one who respects the island and acknowledges that the island has all they need to survive. We see a glimpse of his uniqueness in chapter one: “’ Like candles. Candle bushes. Candle buds.’” (Golding, 20). Later in chapter 3, Simon has disappeared from the group finding sanctuary in another place with all his basic needs. Therefore, Simon can help others because of his security being established, for example, “Simon found for them the fruit they could not reach, pulled off the choicest from up in the foliage, passed them back to the endless, outstretched hands.” (Golding, 40). Simon could be seen as a God-like figure almost like Jesus the way he provides for the group of children. Simon has similarities to Jesus such as providence to people, selflessness, and his inevitable fate in the end
