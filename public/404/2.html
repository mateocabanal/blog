Chapter 2

Questions

When the group decides on a leader, there are two main candidates: Jack and Ralph. However, the two have different approaches and beliefs to leadership. Firstly, Jack uses the pigs as a resource, saying he wants to “hunt pigs to get meat for everybody.” (Golding,25). Additionally, Jack manipulates the fear of the children to form fake protection through statements and actions: “Jack slammed his knife into a trunk and looked round challengingly.” (Golding, 21). Jack also promises that if there were a snake, they would kill it. “But there isn’t a snake-thing. But if there was a snake, we’d hunt it and kill it.” (Golding, 25). On the other hand, Ralph uses a people-pleasing way of leadership, almost like the politicians of today’s world. Ralph uses the fear in an assuring way to the children declaring that “we shall be rescued.” (Golding, 25), without any hard evidence to back up his statement. Instead of providing any valid evidence, he exclaims that “[His] father is in the navy. He said that all islands have been discovered and sooner or later a ship will put in here.” without knowing anything himself. Ralph uses his authority and influences the children into them being rescued, ultimately bringing joy to the depressing situation.

During the first chapter, there are four major forms of symbolism: the conch, piggy’s glasses, Jacks’ knife, and the signal fire. Firstly, the conch is a symbol of order and democracy on the island. Furthermore, the conch is delicate and calls the meetings representing democracy’s fragile nature and its tendency to fail. Secondly, Piggy’s glasses represent rationality within the group because of the glasses being able to light the fire, and piggy being the most intelligent one. Third, Jack’s knife may represent bloodlust and savagery towards the island. To add on, the knife is repeatedly stabbed into multiple trees which may foreshadow the boy’s lack of respect towards the island. Lastly, the signal fire represents their connection to civilization. The more the fire is lit in the later chapters the more civilized and inclined they are to being rescued, and when the fire is not lit the boys are savage and are disconnected from reality.
